Planning:

corporation game is an action - adventure game based off the same ideas and logic as the capcom classic 'megaman.' With this game we're really trying to focus on making a solid platforming - mechanic game based off of fast movements, interesting hard to traverse environments, simple graphics, and a love for destruction of 'the man.'

Platforming:

platforming for this game should generally be easier than our last game 'ying + ging' but I'd say still have a challenge to it due to obstacles like: puzzles, enemies, walljumping, and play-skill testing jumps that refrain from pixel based jumps unless a secret area is involved.

puzzles

puzzles in this game can be boiled down to simple physics based movement puzzles, enemy jump based puzzles where you have to make tricky jumps involving enemy death explosions to propel you toward a position, as well as pattern puzzles of enemies, and/or environment working against you at a certain rythem to try and kill the player with attacks.

double jumping

wall jumpings a great way to get around

wall jumps are going to be a necessary mechanic to master for this game. Wall jumps should be incorporated into most if not all levels and introduced from early on. All solid walls are going to be wall jump-able and all will propel you outward at a constant rate other than specific wall jump puzzle sections.

Wall jump related puzzling and ideas:

a good wall jumping puzzle idea would be to have industrial fans on the walls which propels you further out rather than upwards when you're wall jumping. We could use these to get the player out quicker as well as further horizontally and use it in platforming scenarios mixed with the double jump mechanic for quick gameplay.

sticky goop on the walls could be the opposite of the industrial fans. This will inhibit wall jumping but allow the player to create a bigger degree of verticality. Sticky walls will be able to be basically climbed giving the player better vertical control over sections of wall covered in it.

enemy deaths launch you further!

enemy death explosion platforming puzzles will be less about knowing exactly how to make the jump, but rather how you might be able to get enemies to come your way to make a jump that might not be totally do-able through wall jumping and double jumping alone. Enemies explode in a spectacular fashion making the propulsion from the explosion fling you a little further if you're near. Consider luring enemies closer to a spot you'd like to jump to, use already pre-position enemies for a little bit easier of a jump, or test the players simple mechanic mastery! Combine all the platforming mechanics for all I care.

an example of a great platforming puzzle

a good example of great pattern movement puzzles are 'insides' sonic boom section linked here. inside has an amazing gameplay loop where you use mind control to control multiple 'minions' that can help you move through the level. A great part is the 'sonic boom' section seen at 6 minutes into the video. It uses a menacing sound wave that booms through the area at a constant rythemic pace to give the players a unique and interesting challenge along with amazing sound design that makes you bite your lip.
